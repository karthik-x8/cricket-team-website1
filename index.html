About
    History
    Cricket was invented in England in the 16th century, but modern rules were not used for a long time. It is now played around the world, mainly by countries that were once part of the Commonwealth.
    Game
    Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each. The field is oval or circular, with a rectangular pitch in the middle. The pitch is 22 yards long and 10 feet wide. At each end of the pitch is a wicket, which has two bails balanced on three stumps.
    Teams
    One team bats and tries to score runs, while the other team fields and tries to prevent this. The team with the most runs wins. There are two umpires on the field.
    Clothing
    Cricketers used to wear mainly white clothes and boots, but now many teams wear bright colors. Many cricketers wear boots with metal spikes for extra grip.
    Governing body
    The International Cricket Council is the sport's governing body, and more than 90 countries are members.
    Health benefits
    Cricket can help players develop hand-eye coordination, build and tone muscles, and provide a cardiovascular workout.
